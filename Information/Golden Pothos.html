Golden Pothos

To plant Golden Pothos, choose a location in your home that gets bright, indirect light. Fill a pot with a well-draining potting mix and plant the Golden Pothos in the pot, watering it well. Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Fertilize the plant every 4-6 weeks with a balanced fertilizer. Keep an eye out for pests and take action if necessary. Provide support for the plant if it starts to droop.


Recommended Products

For Golden Pothos, it is recommended to use a well-draining potting mix and a pesticide to prevent infestation of pests like cucumber beetles and squash bugs is important. Regularly staking or caging the plants will help support the heavy fruit and keep the plants upright. To prevent diseases like powdery mildew, it is important to provide good air circulation and avoid overhead watering. Also, it is important to regularly check for rot or decay and remove any affected fruits to avoid spreading the disease to other fruits.
